## The Problem

### Collectors Face:
- **Counterfeits:** 40% of luxury watch market is fake, fears about unknowingly owning/buying fakes
- **Lost Documentation:** Misplaced receipts, certificates, service records = significantly reduced resale value
- **Investment Blindness:** No clear view of portfolio performance, appreciation/depreciation tracking
- **Service Management:** Missed maintenance windows damage value and longevity
- **Resale Difficulty:** Buyers demand proof of authenticity and provenance

### Secondary Market Faces:
- **Trust Deficit:** Buyers skeptical of seller claims without verification
- **Insurance Complexity:** Difficult to prove ownership, value, and authenticity for claims
- **Pricing Uncertainty:** Incomplete documentation = lower selling prices

## Risk Factors

### Adoption Challenges:
- **Trust building:** Luxury market slow to adopt new tech
- **Partnership dependency:** Need service centers to log records
- **Chicken-egg:** Collectors want dealers, dealers want collectors

### Mitigation:
- Focus on high-value early adopters (collectors with $100K+ portfolios)
- Offer free dealer accounts to build supply side
- Partner with auction houses for credibility
- White-glove onboarding for premium tier

### Competition:
- Existing players (WatchBox, Hodinkee) could copy
- Blockchain competitors could emerge

### Mitigation:
- Speed to market (first-mover advantage)
- Network effects (hard to replicate once established)
- Focus on user experience (make blockchain invisible)
